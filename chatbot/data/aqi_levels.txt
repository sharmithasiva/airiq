AQI Categories and Health Impacts (CPCB / WHO Combined):

0–50 (Good): Minimal health risk. Ideal for outdoor activity.
51–100 (Satisfactory): Acceptable air quality. Sensitive individuals should avoid prolonged exposure.
101–200 (Moderate): May cause minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people.
201–300 (Poor): Prolonged exposure may cause respiratory illness and discomfort.
301–400 (Very Poor): Can trigger respiratory effects in healthy people and serious effects in sensitive groups.
401–500 (Severe): Health impact on everyone. Outdoor activity should be completely avoided.

PM2.5 and PM10: Fine particles cause more harm as they penetrate deep into lungs and bloodstream.
